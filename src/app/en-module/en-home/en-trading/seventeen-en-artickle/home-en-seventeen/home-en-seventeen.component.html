Non-market Risks of FOREX

Non-market risks of FOREX can include actions by governments or central banks that indirectly affect the currency market, such as administrative barriers or various restrictions. Additionally, natural disasters, terrorist acts, and other indirect impacts on the market can be considered non-market risks. For example, the terrorist attack in New York on September 11, 2001, immediately impacted the currency market: the value of the US dollar sharply fell. Predicting such risks is impossible, but their influence can be limited by protecting positions and constantly monitoring the market situation.
