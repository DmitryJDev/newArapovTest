Main Participants of the Exchange

The main participants of exchange trading are:

  • traders, who trade independently at their own risk using their own money;
  • brokers, who trade on behalf of clients for a commission fee;
  • market makers, who ensure the execution of the exchange process.

Broker

An intermediary in transactions of buying and selling securities, and exchanges where securities are traded. Their duties:

  • register securities offered for exchange on the market;
  • obtain price quotes;
  • present orders to the market maker; maintain a list of open orders;
  • maintain bid/ask prices;
  • support volatility at their own expense;
  • conduct transactions and transfer ownership rights.

Trader

A professional participant in the securities market engaged in dealer activities. Dealer activities are recognized as the execution of transactions for the purchase and sale of securities in their own name and at their own expense by publicly announcing the purchase and (or) sale prices of certain securities with an obligation to buy and (or) sell certain securities at the prices announced by the person engaging in such activities.

Market Maker

(English: Market maker) — a broker/dealer firm that takes on the risk of purchasing and holding on its accounts the securities of a particular issuer in order to organize their sale. Market makers operate on both the exchange and over-the-counter markets as direct participants in transactions. Typically, market makers act on both sides — as sellers and as buyers.
