Psychological Risks of FOREX Trading

Another important risk associated with trading on the FOREX currency market is psychological factors related to human emotions, hopes, and experiences. When trading on FOREX, every person experiences a multitude of emotions, with the most dangerous being fear and greed. It is the fear of failure and losing money that causes many to hold on to losing positions, hoping that the price movement will change and they can close the position at a profit. Instead of losing a little and preserving their deposit, many wait for changes, increasing their losses. The higher the losses, the harder it is to acknowledge and accept them, which can result in losing the entire deposit.

Greed is no less dangerous on FOREX than fear. Greed drives a person to make impulsive decisions: opening positions irrationally, fearing to miss the opportunity to make a quick profit. After an unsuccessful trade, greed compels trading even more, in hopes of quickly recouping losses, which inevitably leads to new mistakes and so on. In other words, trading under the influence of emotions sooner or later leads to losing all the money.

You can fight psychological risks by remaining cool-headed in any situation, guided by reason rather than emotions when making trades. Only use money for trading on the currency market that you can afford to spend without severe consequences; never trade with borrowed or credit funds. A prepared trading plan and strict adherence to it will help avoid many risks. Always use protective orders. Remember that super profits are directly linked to super risks; the main goal is stability.
