Currency Rate Formation

The exchange rate of each currency, due to the globalization of world financial markets, is practically uniform across the globe at any given moment and is formed based on current quotations offered by the largest market participants. The gradual change in the currency exchange rate is related to the fact that these major participants, based on market factors or their interests, begin to raise or lower the quotation. The main factors contributing to the movement of the exchange rate may include:

  • Changes in economic indicators of the country's economy;
  • Important political events in the world;
  • Major buy-sell transactions by individual market participants;
  • Significant events in related markets (stock, oil, etc.);
  • Changes in the analytical assessment of the currency rate by the majority of participants;
  • Market rumors and changes in market psychology.

Sometimes, certain events cause sharp jumps in the exchange rate, during which prices can fluctuate within a fairly wide range. Such events do not occur often, and the exchange rate changes usually last for a short period.

Margin Trading

Margin trading refers to transactions in the foreign exchange market carried out by allocating a guaranteed deposit from the client's funds (margin) and the leverage provided by the dealer. The essence of it is that the client, by depositing a certain minimum amount, gets the opportunity to operate with amounts significantly exceeding their own funds. This opportunity is provided by brokerage companies, which, in addition to providing market and trading information services, also provide credit lines â€” significant amounts sufficient to enter the market, with which the investor can freely operate by depositing a certain collateral. The system of providing credit against a fixed collateral amount is called leverage, and the fixed collateral amount itself is called margin. This system of entering the global foreign exchange market is called margin trading.

Margin

Margin is the amount of guaranteed collateral under which leverage is provided.

Leverage

Leverage is a system of providing credit against a collateral amount for operations in the international foreign exchange market. Leverage is set by the market-making bank and effectively determines the ratio between the collateral amount and the allocated loan capital.
