Introduction to the Exchange

What is Traded on the Exchange

Exchange

А legal entity that ensures the regular functioning of an organized market for exchange goods, currencies, securities, and derivative financial instruments. Trading is conducted with standard contracts or lots, the sizes of which are regulated by exchange regulatory documents.

Before the computerization era, transactions were mostly arranged orally. Now, trading largely takes place electronically using specialized programs, and many exchanges have abandoned trading floors. Brokers, in their own interests or in the interests of clients, enter orders to buy or sell exchange goods into trading systems. These orders are matched with counter orders from other traders. The exchange keeps track of executed transactions, organizes and guarantees settlements (clearing), and provides a mechanism for "delivery versus payment" interaction.

Usually, exchanges charge a commission fee for each transaction executed through their assistance, which is their main source of income. Other sources may include membership fees, fees for access to trading, and the sale of exchange information.

Functions of the Exchange:

  • Organization of exchange trading;
  • Establishment of trading rules, including standards for goods traded through the exchange;
  • Development of standard contracts;
  • Quotation;
  • Settlement (arbitration) of disputes;
  • Information activities;
  • Providing certain guarantees for the performance of obligations by trading participants.

Classification of Major Exchanges:

  • Commodity exchanges
  • Stock exchanges
  • Currency exchanges
  • Derivatives exchanges

Commodity Exchange:

On a commodity exchange, there is always an opportunity to buy or sell real goods, despite the fact that modern exchanges primarily represent futures and options markets. Futures are contracts for the delivery of real goods.

Stock Exchange:

A stock exchange is a financial institution that ensures the regular functioning of an organized market for securities. In each market economy country, there is a national stock exchange where trading in stocks, bonds, and other financial instruments takes place.

Currency Exchange:

A currency exchange is a platform for the free buying and selling of national currencies at established rates, which are formed by supply and demand in the market.

Futures Exchange:

A futures exchange (or derivatives exchange) specializes in trading futures contracts — agreements for the future delivery of exchange goods or securities.

These types of exchanges perform important functions in financial markets, ensuring liquidity, protecting the interests of market participants, and facilitating efficient price formation.
